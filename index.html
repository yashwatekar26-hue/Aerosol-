AJP // EVOLUTIONARY MAP
Phase 01 // Baseline Fluidics

Aerodynamic Liquid Baseline

Source Framework: Wilkinson & Smith (2019)

Core Mechanism: Utilizes pneumatic/ultrasonic atomization to turn liquid metal nanoparticle inks into an aerosol mist. Focuses the mist using a continuous ring of Sheath Gas Collimation to keep the nozzle from clogging via material contact.

System Bottleneck: Highly volatile fluid dynamics. Minor shifts in ink viscosity lead to systemic, immediate nozzle clogging.

Phase 02 // Structural Complexity

Multi-Material Polymer Matrices

Source Framework: Hwang et al.

Core Mechanism: Expands processing capabilities past simple metals by engineering a Binary Co-Solvent Kinetic system, stabilizing the evaporation rates of complex liquid polymer blends mid-flight.

System Bottleneck: Liquid droplet splashing upon substrate impact warps fine geometries, trapping structural resolution at a strict 10-micrometer floor.

↓
Phase 03 // Liquid-Free Shift

The Dry Electrostatic Detour

Source Framework: Choi & Jung (Seoul National University)

Core Mechanism: Eliminates liquid entirely. Uses spark discharge to generate pure, dry metallic nanoparticles. Guides the charged aerosol particles via an active Electrostatic Lens Array instead of standard physical gas nozzles.

System Bottleneck: Achieves unmatched sub-micron precision (<1 µm) with zero clogging, but is fundamentally restricted to pure elemental metals; cannot print complex functional polymers.

System synthesis // The Research Gap

The Unresolved Structural Gap

Liquid systems handle complex multi-material polymers but are ruined by low resolution and nozzle clogging. Dry systems achieve near-perfect nanoscale accuracy but cannot process polymers.

Core Research Question: Can localized electrostatic lens fields be deployed to guide a liquid polymer aerosol mist to sub-micron scales without initiating nozzle clog points?
